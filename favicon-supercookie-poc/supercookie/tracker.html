The cat righting reflex is one of nature's most elegant biomechanical solutions. When a cat falls, it can rotate its body mid-air to land on its feet in as little as 0.3 seconds, even from a height of just 30 centimeters.

The physics involves conservation of angular momentum. The cat tucks its front legs (reducing moment of inertia) while extending its back legs, allowing differential rotation of the front and back halves of its body independently.
